Note: 'Sig1' = current ephemeris uncertainty; 'Sig2' = ephemeris uncertainty in two days. This is a good guide as to how important it is to get a particular object. If the current uncertainty is ten arcseconds, but it'll be twenty degrees in two days, you'd best get it soon. If it'll only have grown to an arcminute, it may not be so important. (Though it's only a guide. The object may get faint or close to the sun or too far south or north for you during those two days. Which is why my work here is not yet done.)
